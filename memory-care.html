Memory Care Communities in the North State Region

Specialized support for seniors living with Alzheimer's, dementia, and other forms of cognitive decline.

Memory care is a form of senior living designed exclusively for individuals with Alzheimer's disease, dementia, or other types of cognitive impairment. These communities offer secured environments, higher staffing ratios, and programming specifically designed for the cognitive and sensory needs of people with memory loss.

A memory care placement is often one of the most emotionally difficult decisions a family makes. We approach this with care and patience — and we personally know the communities in our network. Contact us when you're ready to talk.

What Makes Memory Care Different

  • Secured environment — controlled access prevents wandering and keeps residents safe
  • Specialized staff training — caregivers trained in dementia communication, de-escalation, and behavioral support
  • Higher staffing ratios — more staff per resident than standard assisted living
  • Purpose-built programming — activities designed around cognitive engagement, sensory stimulation, and reminiscence therapy
  • Structured daily routines — consistency reduces anxiety and behavioral symptoms common in dementia
  • Family support resources — education, caregiver support groups, and regular care conferences

When Is It Time for Memory Care?

  • Wandering or becoming disoriented — indoors or outdoors
  • Significant behavioral changes — agitation, aggression, or severe sundowning
  • Inability to be safely supervised at home
  • Standard assisted living community is no longer able to safely manage care needs
  • A diagnosis of moderate to severe Alzheimer's or related dementia
  • Caregiver safety is at risk

What to Look for in a Memory Care Community

  • Staff trained specifically in dementia care — not general caregiving
  • A secured, thoughtfully designed environment (not just a locked door)
  • An activity program designed for cognitive engagement — not generic entertainment
  • Clear family communication policies and care conferencing schedules
  • Behavioral support capabilities — how does the community handle difficult behaviors without over-medicating?
  • Clean state inspection record — check at ccld.dss.ca.gov
