Independent Living Communities in the North State Region

For active seniors who are ready to simplify — and thrive in a community of peers.

Independent living communities are designed for older adults who are largely self-sufficient but want to stop managing a home, enjoy daily meals and activities, and live among peers. There is no medical or personal care staff providing hands-on assistance — residents manage their own daily routines, with the added comfort of safety features and a built-in social environment.

What's Included in Independent Living

  • Private apartment or cottage (studio, one-bedroom, or two-bedroom options)
  • Restaurant-style dining — typically two or three meals per day
  • Housekeeping and linen service
  • Scheduled transportation to medical appointments, shopping, and outings
  • A full activity and social calendar — fitness classes, clubs, cultural events, outings
  • 24-hour emergency call system
  • Utilities, basic cable, and Wi-Fi included
  • Maintenance-free living — no yard work, repairs, or home upkeep

Who Independent Living is Right For

Independent living works best for a senior who can independently manage all activities of daily living — bathing, dressing, medication management, mobility — but who is ready to let go of home ownership and enjoy a simpler, more social lifestyle. Many residents move in by choice, not out of necessity, and find it one of the best decisions they've made.

It is not appropriate for seniors who need hands-on care, medication management, or memory support. Independent living communities are not licensed to provide those services — if care needs emerge, a move to assisted living may be required.

Questions to Ask on a Tour

  • What is the process if my care needs increase — can I transition to assisted living on this campus?
  • What activities are offered, and how often do they change?
  • What is the community's policy on guests, family visits, and pets?
  • How are annual rate increases handled?
