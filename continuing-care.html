Continuing Care Retirement Communities in the North State Region

One community, every level of care — for seniors and couples planning ahead.

Continuing Care Retirement Communities — also called CCRCs or Life Plan Communities — offer independent living, assisted living, and often memory care or skilled nursing all on one campus. The defining benefit is continuity: as a resident's needs change over time, they can transition between care levels without leaving their community, their friends, or their established routines.

Why Families Choose Continuing Care

  • One decision, not several — move in once, and the community can care for your loved one as needs evolve
  • Couples flexibility — one partner can live in independent living while the other receives assisted living or memory care on the same campus
  • Peace of mind — families know a plan is in place regardless of what happens medically
  • Community continuity — residents keep the same friends, dining room, and community even as care levels change

Important Considerations

CCRCs vary significantly in how they structure their contracts and fees. The three main contract types are:

Type A — Life Care

Higher upfront entrance fee, but covers all future care at little to no additional charge. Provides the most financial predictability long-term.

Type B — Modified

Entrance fee plus discounted rates for higher levels of care when needed. A middle ground between full life care and fee-for-service.

Type C — Fee for Service

Lower or no entrance fee. You pay market rate for each level of care as you need it. Less upfront cost, more variable cost over time.

Rental Model

No entrance fee — monthly rates only. Most common in the North State market. More flexible but less long-term price certainty.

Most communities in the North State region operate on a rental model rather than a traditional CCRC entrance-fee structure. This makes them more accessible but means there is less long-term cost protection than a full life-care contract provides.

Questions to Ask a Continuing Care Community

  • What type of contract do you offer — life care, modified, fee-for-service, or rental?
  • Is there an entrance fee, and is any portion refundable?
  • What is the process for transitioning from one care level to another?
  • What happens if a resident's funds are depleted — can they remain in the community?
  • Can couples in different care levels share meals and spend time together?
  • What is the community's occupancy rate and financial stability?
